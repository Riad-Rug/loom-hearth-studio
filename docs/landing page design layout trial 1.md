# Competitor Analysis & Layout Recommendations for Loom & Hearth Studio

## Part 1  What the Competition Does (Ranked by Design Quality)

### Tier 1  Best in Class

**Revival Rugs**  The benchmark for rug e-commerce UX globally. Three things no competitor matches: **(1)** A **Room Visualizer** ("See It In Your Room") letting customers upload their own photo and place a rug in it  this single feature removes the biggest conversion barrier in rug buying; **(2)** A **Rug Finder Quiz** that guides indecisive shoppers through 45 questions to a matched product; **(3)** **10,000+ reviews** woven directly into the product sections rather than isolated on a separate page. Their navigation mega-menu organises by Size, Room, Colour, and Washability simultaneously  every entry point a buyer might think of.

**Zarabe**  The most structurally disciplined Moroccan boutique. Their homepage flows as: Hero  Shop by Style (3 cards)  Brand story statement  Most Wanted products  "As Seen In" media logos  Value pillars  Testimonials  Core values  Custom rugs  Journal. Every section earns its place. The "RUGS INSPIRED BY ARTISTS, CRAFTED BY ARTISANS" positioning is the sharpest one-line differentiator in the Moroccan space.

### Tier 2  Strong at Specific Things

**Kenza & Co**  Best hero execution in the Moroccan category: Atlas Mountains photo, artisan copy, single focused CTA. "Bring warmth, story, and soul into your home" is excellent headline writing. They use scarcity well ("Only 1 in stock") without being aggressive. Weakness: heavy discount signage (5470% off) damages premium positioning.

**Baba Kech**  Best artisan storytelling: the "From Hands to Homes" narrative with founder Haj Khalid's name and face creates genuine human connection. Trust signals appear before product  reviews, then story, then shop. Weakness: photography mixes lifestyle and studio inconsistently.

**Beni Souk**  Best in-category filtering: tribe  colour  size  style creates every discovery pathway a Moroccan rug buyer needs. Educational content about tribal symbolism establishes authority. Weakness: the homepage leads with text rather than imagery.

**Bohemia Marrakech**  Most conversion-engineered of the Moroccan boutiques: urgency messaging, scarcity badges, promo codes, free worldwide shipping. 100,000+ rugs sold counter provides instant credibility. Weakness: the discount-heavy aesthetic undermines premium positioning and trains buyers to wait for sales.

**Authentic Moroccan Rugs (Adrar)**  Best named-artisan approach: "Zahra, Saida, and Fatima" in the hero copy makes the sourcing story immediately human and specific. "The Quiet Beauty of Adrar" as a headline is genuinely editorial.

### Tier 3  Functional but Undifferentiated

**Exquisite Rugs**  Sophisticated trade programme, strong dealer registration pathway, good custom sizing (up to 1530ft). Design is clean but cold  blue-grey palette with minimal imagery.

**Beni Souk** / **Bohemia Marrakech**  Both treat the homepage as a product catalogue rather than an editorial destination. Effective but not memorable.

## Part 3  Homepage Layout Recommendation

The current homepage uses a single hero card with text. Based on what converts in this category, here is the recommended section-by-section layout:

### Section 1  FULL-WIDTH HERO (above the fold, edge-to-edge)

**What competitors do:** Every top performer opens with a full-bleed atmospheric image.
**What to do:** Remove the rounded card. Let a full-width bazaar or interior photo fill the screen edge-to-edge with a dark gradient overlay at the bottom. White headline and two CTAs over the image.

```text
[FULL BLEED IMAGE  bazaar or Moroccan interior with rug prominent]

                 Handcrafted Moroccan rugs.
              Selected in person, one at a time.

        [Shop the collection]   [Read how we source]
```

The right-hand side can hold a floating card showing "52 pieces  Free to US, Canada & Australia" for trust context without cluttering the hero.

### Section 2  TRUST STRIP (4 icons, horizontal)

**What competitors do:** Zarabe, Kenza, Beni Souk all use an icon strip immediately after the hero to answer the "who are you and why trust you?" question.

```text
[] One of one    [] Colour verified before payment    [] Ships free to US/CA/AU    [🏺] Sourced in Marrakech
```

No card container  4 columns directly on the white background. Compact, scannable, credibility-building.

### Section 3  SHOP BY CATEGORY (3 full-height photo cards)

**What competitors do:** Every competitor provides visual category entry points. L&H has only text-based category links.

```text
[RUGS IMAGE]              [POUFS & PILLOWS IMAGE]        [VINTAGE IMAGE]
Hand-knotted rugs         Rug-made poufs & pillows       One-of-one vintage pieces
from $470                 from $215                      from $630
[Browse rugs ]           [Browse poufs ]               [Browse vintage ]
```

3-column grid, full-height images (400px), title + starting price + CTA overlaid at the bottom of each card.

### Section 4  FEATURED PIECES (4 cards, no sidebar, full-width)

**What competitors do:** Homepage product features drive immediate click-to-shop. Currently L&H has no homepage product grid.

4 product cards showing the best-photographed pieces currently in the collection. Same card style as the shop page. Heading: *"From the current collection"* with "See all 52 pieces " as a right-aligned link.

### Section 5  THE SOURCING STORY (alternating layout  no card)

**What Authentic Moroccan Rugs, Zarabe and Baba Kech do:** A single striking image paired with editorial copy. This is where L&H is genuinely differentiated  the sourcing story is better than any competitor. It should be on the homepage.

```text

                                   |
```

No card wrapper. Two equal columns. Image left, text right. Dark beige or white background.

### Section 6  SHOP BY COLOUR (horizontal scroll strip)

**What every competitor has:** Colour is how most rug buyers actually think. "I need something warm/ivory/terracotta."

A horizontal scrolling strip of 68 colour swatches with labels:


Each swatch links to a filtered shop view. Compact, no card, sits on a light beige band.

### Section 10  CLOSING CTA (dark, full-width)

**What the best pages do:** End with a conversion invitation.


## Part 4  Shop Page Additional Recommendations

Beyond the homepage, the shop page needs two things competitors consistently offer that L&H is missing:

**1. Shop by Colour filter**  Currently the shop has Category, Price, and Size. Colour is the most common entry point for rug buyers ("I need something ivory", "I'm looking for terracotta"). Add it as a 5th dropdown or as a colour swatch strip above the grid.

**2. Second hover image on product cards**  Every top-tier competitor (Revival, Exquisite Rugs) shows a styled/room-context image on card hover. The first image is the overhead product shot; on hover it swaps to the rug in a room. Massively improves click-through without adding page weight.

## Part 5  One Feature Worth Building

**"How will this rug look in my room?"  A Lightweight Colour Context Tool**

Revival Rugs' Room Visualizer is the most powerful conversion tool in the category. A full AR implementation is expensive, but a simpler version is achievable: show each rug in 3 room contexts (light room, warm room, dark room) as static photography  similar to what the trade page's light comparison widget already does. Label them "Natural light", "Warm interior", "Evening". This addresses the #1 anxiety of online rug buyers ("will the colour work in my space?") without any AR technology.
